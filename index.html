Why a plain, single-column layout wins

Many resume builders sell flashy two-column templates with icons, sidebars, and graphics. They look sharp to a human — and get scrambled or dropped entirely by applicant tracking systems, which most companies use to screen resumes before a person ever sees them.

CVCraft deliberately uses a single-column, text-first layout: the same structure recommended by career coaches for ATS compatibility, styled cleanly enough that it still looks sharp to a human reader too.

Frequently asked questions

Is CVCraft really free?

Yes. There's no account, no trial period, and no watermark on the exported PDF. The tool runs entirely in your browser, so there's no server cost tied to how many resumes you generate.

Is my information stored anywhere?

Your draft is saved locally in your own browser's storage so you don't lose progress on refresh — it's never transmitted to a server. Clearing your browser data or using a different device will not carry your draft over.

What does "ATS-friendly" actually mean?

Applicant tracking systems are the software many companies use to scan and rank resumes before a recruiter reads them. They work by extracting text — job titles, dates, skills — from your file. A resume built as flattened images or complex multi-column graphics often gets misread or garbled. CVCraft's PDF export uses real, structured text in a single-column layout so that extraction is reliable.

Can I make more than one version for different jobs?

Yes — there's no limit on exports. A common approach is to keep your draft general, then adjust your summary and bullet points slightly before exporting for each application.
